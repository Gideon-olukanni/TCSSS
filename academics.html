Academics

Three Pathways, One Standard

Every student works toward the same standard of excellence — through whichever stream fits where they're headed next.

Core subjects — English Language, Mathematics and Civic Education — run throughout all three senior secondary years. Alongside them, students choose one academic stream, which shapes the rest of their subject combination through to their final examinations.

The Three Streams

Choose a Path, Not a Ceiling

Each stream is a complete preparation for WAEC and NECO — and for what comes after.

Stream 01

Sciences

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology
  • Further Mathematics
  • Agricultural Science

Leads toward medicine, engineering and other STEM fields at university.

Stream 02

Arts & Humanities

  • Literature in English
  • Government
  • History
  • CRS / IRS
  • Yoruba Language

Leads toward law, the humanities and social sciences at university.

Stream 03

Commercial

  • Financial Accounting
  • Commerce
  • Economics
  • Office Practice
  • Marketing

Leads toward business, accounting and economics at university.

English Language, Mathematics and Civic Education are compulsory across all three streams — every student sits these regardless of stream.

External Examinations

WAEC & NECO

For readers unfamiliar with the Nigerian education system — what these qualifications actually are.

WAEC

The West African Examinations Council administers the Senior School Certificate Examination across several West African countries. It is the standardized exit examination that marks the end of secondary education — broadly comparable to a national high-school qualifying exam elsewhere in the world.

NECO

The National Examinations Council administers Nigeria's own version of the same Senior School Certificate Examination. Students typically sit both WAEC and NECO before completing secondary school, and universities — in Nigeria and abroad — use these results to evaluate a student's secondary education.
